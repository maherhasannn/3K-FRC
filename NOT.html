What is a Notice of Trustee Sale?

A Notice of Trustee Sale is a legal document issued to inform a borrower that their property is scheduled to be sold at a public auction due to default on a mortgage or deed of trust. This notice follows a Notice of Default and signifies that the lender is moving forward with foreclosure proceedings. The document includes details about the auction date, time, and location, and provides information on how the borrower can prevent the sale by curing the default or negotiating with the lender.

Understanding and Responding to a Notice of Trustee Sale

What It Is:

A Notice of Trustee Sale is a legal document indicating that a property is scheduled for auction due to default on a mortgage or deed of trust. It marks a critical step in the foreclosure process, providing details about the auction and options for avoiding the sale.

Reasons for Receiving a Notice of Trustee Sale:

  • Missed Mortgage Payments: Failure to make required loan payments.
  • Unresolved Default: Failing to address issues from a prior Notice of Default.
  • Violation of Loan Terms: Breaching other conditions of the loan agreement.
  • Unpaid Property Taxes: Delinquency in property tax payments.
  • Bankruptcy: Issues arising from bankruptcy proceedings affecting loan repayment.

Steps to Take After Receiving a Notice of Trustee Sale:

Review the Notice: Carefully read the document to understand the details and deadlines.

Contact Your Lender: Discuss possible solutions or options to prevent the auction.

Seek Legal Advice: Consult with an attorney to explore legal options and understand your rights.

Explore Loan Modification: Investigate opportunities for loan modification or repayment plans.

Prepare for Auction: If necessary, prepare for the auction by understanding the process and options for relocation.
